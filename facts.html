Elephant Facts

Elephants are one of the best-loved and most recognised animals on the planet. Famed for their problem-solving intelligence and incredible loyalty to each other, they are also subjects of mystery, with many aspects of their lives still unknown. Here, are some great elephant facts.

  1. Until 2021, African elephants were considered a single species, with forest elephants a subspecies. However, recent studies showed their DNA differed significantly, and in 2021, the two were recognised as distinct species.
  2. It is estimated that about 50,000 Asian elephants survive today.
  3. Approximately 13,000 of the 50,000 surviving Asian elephants are maintained in human care.
  4. Elephants live in matriarchal groups.
  5. Herds are a multigenerational society and they do everything together.
  6. The African elephant is the largest living land mammal.
  7. Elephants have hair all over their bodies including eyelashes.
  8. Only male Asian elephants have large tusks.
  9. Female Asian elephants have small tushes that rarely extend beyond their lip line.
  10. The tusks of elephants grow throughout their life.
  11. Tusks can weigh over 200 pounds each.
  12. An elephant’s tooth can weigh as much as three kilograms.
  13. Elephants have a slow pulse of 27 pulses per minute.
  14. The elephant can’t jump; they are the only mammal that can’t.
  15. The elephant is the national animal of Thailand.
  16. The elephant is pregnant for an average of 22 months.
  17. On average, baby elephants weigh 200–300 pounds at birth.
  18. Elephant calves can stand within 20 minutes of birth.
  19. Elephants purr like cats do, as a means of communication.
  20. Elephants are smart and have been known to learn more than 60 commands.
  21. Normal daily water consumption is about 25–50 gallons per animal, or 100–200 liters.
  22. An elephant’s trunk can hold 2.5 gallons of water.
  23. Elephant trunks are extremely sensitive – they contain no bones.
  24. The elephant trunk serves as a nose, a hand, an extra foot, a signaling device, and a tool for gathering food, siphoning water, dusting, digging, and much more.
  25. Elephants don’t drink with their trunks as if it were a straw, but instead use them as a glass holding the water and pouring it into their mouths.
  26. Elephants can swim and they use their trunk to breathe like a snorkel in deep water.
  27. Elephants can live up to 70 years.
  28. Sadly, in captivity, the lifespan of an elephant is much shorter, at a median age of only 17.
  29. By definition elephants don't run.
  30. The oldest known elephant in the world is thought to be an Asian elephant who lived to age 89 in captivity.
  31. Trunks have no joints or bones.
  32. Elephants think we are cute; they look at humans like we look at puppies.
  33. Elephants are usually left- or right-tusked.
  34. Elephant tusks are enlarged incisor teeth that first appear when they are around 2 years old.
  35. Tusks are used for feeding, such as pulling bark off trees or digging up roots, and as a defense in fighting.
  36. An elephant’s skin is 2.5 cm thick in most places.
  37. They can get sub burnt.
  38. They can reach speeds of 40km/h.
  39. The folds and wrinkles in their skin can retain up to 10 times more water than flat skin, which helps cool them down.
  40. Elephants keep their skin clean and protect themselves from sunburn by taking regular dust and mud baths.
  41. The phrase “an elephant never forgets” comes from their fantastic long-term memory.
  42. Their ears are not just for hearing—they also help regulate body temperature. Flapping their massive ears cools the blood, which then circulates through their body to cool them down.
  43. Elephants have around 150,000 muscle units in their trunk, which serves as a nose, a hand, an extra foot, a signaling device, and a tool for gathering food, siphoning water, dusting, digging, and more.
  44. Elephants are constantly eating
  45. Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors, a sign of self-awareness that they share with humans, dolphins, and great apes.
  46. Their brains can weigh up to 5.4kg
  47. They have mourning rituals. Elephants have been observed grieving over the bodies of their dead, sometimes even returning to the spot years later.
  48. Some elephants have been known to express empathy by comforting other distressed elephants or even helping humans in need.
  49. Elephants do wrap their trunks around each other to reassure and to greet other elephants.
  50. They can "hear" through their feet! Elephants pick up seismic vibrations through the sensitive pads of their feet, allowing them to detect distant thunderstorms or even movements of other herds.
  51. Despite a ban on international sales of ivory in 1989, elephants are still being targeted for their ivory.
  52. Elephant poaching for ivory has destroyed 90% of elephant population in the past century
  53. Elephants are scared of bees.
  54. There are three subspecies of Asian elephant: the Indian (or mainland), the Sumatran, and the Sri Lankan.
  55. Elephants have poor eyesight.
